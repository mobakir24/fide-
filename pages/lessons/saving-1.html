What is Saving?

📖 5 min read
🎯 3 quiz questions

What is Saving?

Saving means keeping some of your money instead of spending all of it. Think of it like this — every time you get money, you put some of it in a safe place to use later.

Imagine you get 10 coins every week. If you spend all 10, at the end of the month you have 0. But if you save 3 coins every week, at the end of the month you have 12 coins saved — that's more than a whole week of coins!

💡 Real Life Example

Ahmed gets 20 TL every week from his parents. He spends 15 TL on snacks and saves 5 TL. After 4 weeks, he has saved 20 TL — enough to buy the book he really wanted!

Why is Saving Important?

Saving is one of the most powerful things you can do with money. Here is why:

Key Points
→ Buy bigger things: Some things cost more than you have right now. Saving lets you buy them later.
→ Handle emergencies: If something unexpected happens, your savings can help you.
→ Feel secure: Knowing you have money saved makes you feel safe and confident.
→ Reach your goals: Want a new bike? A game? Saving is the path to get there.
🌱
Fun Fact
If you save just 1 TL every day, in one year you will have 365 TL! Small amounts add up to big amounts over time. This is called the power of consistency.

How to Start Saving

Starting to save is simple. You don't need a lot of money — you just need a plan:

3 Simple Steps
1. Decide how much to save — Even 10% of what you get is a great start. If you get 10 TL, save 1 TL.
2. Put it away immediately — The moment you get money, save your portion before you spend anything.
3. Don't touch it — Your savings are for your goal, not for everyday spending.
💡 Try This at Home

Get three jars or envelopes. Label them: Save, Spend, and Give. Every time you get money, put some in each one. This is called the "Three Jar Method" and it's used by smart people all over the world!
